Fire Detection Cube Sat

I designed a CubeSat meant to detect fire using computer vision, take photos, and send data to a ground station to aid first responders. This was made during the outbreak of fires in California and around the world.

1. Project Overview

The project focused on creating a 1U CubeSat capable of identifying the location and size of fires. It autonomously processes imagery to filter out non-relevant data before transmission to save bandwidth and power.

2. Objective

We wanted to make a 1U CubeSat that could report needed information about fires to aid humanitarian efforts.

3. Methodology

We used a Raspberry Pi as the motherboard and added a photo module. We coded the satellite to take photos periodically, ensuring full coverage of the earth through its orbit. It includes logic to identify areas of fire and delete images not containing fire, sleeping while not taking photos to save battery. A solar panel was added to recharge the battery.

4. Results & Outcome

We won "Best Presentation" in the challenge we submitted it to. The system successfully demonstrated the concept of autonomous fire detection from orbit.

5. Next Steps

I would like to expand this into a 2U or bigger Sat so that it could improve universal access to satellite imagery to aid humanitarian efforts.

Key Features

  • Computer Vision for Fire Detection
  • Solar Panel Integration
  • Relay between satellite and ground station
  • Smart Data storage optimization
  • Power management (sleep mode)

Technologies Used

Raspberry Pi Python OpenCV Electrical Engineering
